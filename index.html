What is Flag Football?

Flag football is a safe, non-contact version of American football. Instead of tackling, defenders pull a player’s flag belt to stop the play. Teams try to move the ball down the field and score a touchdown in the end zone.

🏈
What players do
Throw, catch, run routes, hand off, defend passes, and pull flags.
🚩
How a play stops
A defender pulls the ball carrier’s flag, a pass falls incomplete, or a score happens.
🧠
What kids learn
Teamwork, spacing, routes, passing timing, defense, and sportsmanship.
😊
Why it’s fun
Fast plays, lots of movement, easier safety rules, and everyone can join in.

Simple kid-friendly game flow

1. Start the play
The center snaps the ball to the quarterback.
2. Choose a move
The offense can pass, hand off, or run.
3. Defend safely
The defense covers players and tries to pull the flag.
4. Score!
If the offense reaches the end zone, that is a touchdown.

Common Fouls

These are common kid-friendly examples teachers can explain. Exact penalties can vary by school or league, so coaches can customize the wording later.

🚫🖐️
Flag Guarding
Using hands, arms, or the ball to block a defender from pulling your flag.
🚫💥
Illegal Contact
Pushing, tackling, or rough contact instead of safe flag football play.
🚫⏱️
Offside
Starting across the line before the ball is snapped.
🚫🌀
Spinning Into Contact
Turning or jumping into defenders in a dangerous way.
🚫🤾
Illegal Screen / Block
Getting in someone’s way with the body when the rules do not allow blocking.
🚫🎯
Pass Interference
Grabbing or bumping a receiver too early while the ball is in the air.

5v5 Positions

This tab gives a simple overview of the main jobs kids may hear about in class or practice.

Quarterback (QB)
Gets the snap and decides to throw, hand off, or run.
Center
Snaps the ball to start the play.
Wide Receiver (WR)
Runs routes and catches passes.
Running Back (RB)
Can take handoffs, run, or catch short passes.
Rusher / Defensive Back / Safety / Linebacker
These defenders rush the QB, cover receivers, help in the middle, and protect deep passes depending on the scheme.
